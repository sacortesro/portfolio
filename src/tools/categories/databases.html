Databases

Bioinformatics databases with multiple purposes.

GenBank NCBI
General DB

Comprehensive public database of nucleotide sequences and supporting bibliographic and biological annotation.

Key Applications:

  • Access to a vast repository of DNA sequences from various organisms
  • Sequence retrieval for comparative genomics and evolutionary studies
  • Gene identification and functional annotation

UniProt
Protein DB

Comprehensive resource for protein sequence and functional information, including detailed annotations and cross-references to other databases.

Key Applications:

  • Protein sequence retrieval for bioinformatics analyses
  • Functional annotation and domain identification
  • Cross-referencing with other biological databases

EMBL-EBI
General DB

A comprehensive resource for bioinformatics data and services, including a wide range of databases and tools for genomic, proteomic, and structural biology research.

Key Applications:

  • Access to various biological databases (e.g., Ensembl, ArrayExpress)
  • Tools for sequence analysis, structural biology, and systems biology
  • Data integration and visualization services

Pfam
Protein Families DB

Database of protein families and domains, providing multiple sequence alignments and hidden Markov models for each family.

Key Applications:

  • Identification of protein domains and families
  • Functional annotation based on domain architecture
  • Evolutionary studies of protein families

KEGG
Metabolic Pathways DB

Comprehensive database resource for understanding high-level functions and utilities of the biological system, such as the cell, the organism, and the ecosystem, from molecular-level information.

Key Applications:

  • Pathway mapping for metabolic and signaling pathways
  • Functional annotation of genes and proteins
  • Integration of genomic, chemical, and systemic functional information
KEGG Documentation My KEGG Scripts
BRENDA
Enzyme DB

Comprehensive enzyme information system providing data on enzyme function, structure, and kinetics.

Key Applications:

  • Retrieval of enzyme data for biochemical research
  • Functional annotation of enzymes in metabolic pathways
  • Kinetic data for modeling enzymatic reactions

DDBJ
Nucleotide DB

DNA Data Bank of Japan (DDBJ) is a public database for nucleotide sequences and related information, collaborating with GenBank and EMBL.

Key Applications:

  • Access to a comprehensive repository of nucleotide sequences
  • Sequence submission and retrieval for genomic studies
  • Collaboration with international nucleotide sequence databases
